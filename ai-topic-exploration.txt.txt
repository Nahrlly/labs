My Teach-Back
our eyes are high speed sensors which transmit information to the brain which is then compiled and turned into images. As light travels through, it is compiled in different ways, in the end turning into a signal that the brain can use. Before the brain can read this signal, the eyes also compress the signal, reducing the bandwidth while still retaining the information.
